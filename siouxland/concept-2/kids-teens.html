Care that fits their world

Kids and teens are busy, and their smiles are still growing and changing. We meet them where they are with gentle, proven care and a friendly team that helps them feel at ease from the very first visit.

We suggest a first check-up around age 7 so we can watch how the smile is developing. That early look often means simpler care later, and there is no pressure to start treatment early. When the time is right, options include braces, clear braces, and Invisalign. We also offer airway-focused care, an approach designed to support healthy breathing and sleep as the jaw develops.

Common questions

Ask Dr. Williams a question
Isn't my child too young?
Probably not for a first look. We suggest a check-up around age 7 so we can watch how the smile is developing, and in most cases the answer is simply to keep an eye on things. There is no pressure to start treatment early, and the visit gives you clear answers.
Will braces get in the way of sports or band?
Kids stay active in braces and aligners all the time, including sports and playing instruments. For contact sports we will talk about protecting the mouth so your child can keep doing what they love, and most students adjust quickly.
How long does treatment take?
It varies from smile to smile, since every child is different. At your visit we will give you a clear, honest estimate based on what we see, so you know what to expect before anything begins.
